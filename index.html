Naegele's rule: EDD = LMP + 280 days (40 weeks). Cycle length adjustment adds or subtracts days relative to the 28-day baseline. For cycles ≠28 days: EDD = LMP + 280 + (cycle − 28) days.

Ultrasound dating: Early ultrasound (before 14 weeks) is more accurate than LMP for EDD assignment. ACOG recommends using US dating if it differs from LMP by >7 days in the first trimester or >14 days in the second trimester.

Formula (Mari 2000)
Median PSV = e^(2.31 + 0.046 × GA)
MoM = Measured PSV ÷ Median PSV

GA in decimal weeks. Derived from exponential regression on the Mari 2000 normative dataset. Valid from 18–40 weeks.

Interpretation Thresholds

MoMInterpretationAction
< 1.29NormalRoutine follow-up
1.29 – 1.49BorderlineRepeat in 1–2 wk
≥ 1.50Moderate–severe anemiaConsider FBS / IUT

Anti-Kell alloimmunization

Anti-Kell suppresses erythropoiesis. MCA PSV thresholds still apply, but anemia may occur at lower MoM. Lower threshold for FBS is appropriate. Standard MoM cutoffs may underestimate risk.

Reference: Mari G, Deter RL, Carpenter RL, et al. Noninvasive diagnosis by Doppler ultrasonography of fetal anemia due to maternal red-cell alloimmunization. N Engl J Med. 2000;342(1):9–14. — Sensitivity 100% for moderate–severe anemia at MoM ≥1.5; false-positive rate 12%.

Technique note: Sample near the origin of the MCA from the circle of Willis. Angle of insonation <20° (ideally 0°). Avoid fetal breathing movements. Use the highest measurable PSV from at least three consecutive waveforms.

Workup Checklist

When HC < −2 SD, consider:
• Fetal MRI brain (best after 28–30 wks)
• TORCH serology (CMV, Toxo, Rubella, Zika)
• Chromosomal microarray / exome if indicated
• Detailed anatomy scan (associated anomalies)
• Serial growth scans q3–4 weeks
• Fetal neurology / genetics referral

Reference: Chervenak FA, Jeanty P, Cantraine F, et al. The diagnosis of fetal microcephaly. Am J Obstet Gynecol. 1984;149(5):512–517. PMID: 6742021. — Normative HC data from 396 normal singleton pregnancies; −2 SD used as the diagnostic threshold for microcephaly.

Important caveat: HC must be interpreted with overall fetal growth context. Isolated mild reduction (−2 to −2.5 SD) with normal biometry and anatomy may represent a constitutional variant. Severe or progressive microcephaly (<−3 SD) warrants comprehensive workup regardless of associated findings.

Clinical Notes

Normal trend: UA PI decreases progressively with advancing gestation as placental villous vascularity increases and resistance falls.

Elevated PI: Suggests increased placental resistance. In FGR, up to 25% show elevated UA PI. Progressive worsening → AEDF → REDF indicates deterioration.

AEDF/REDF: PI-based percentiles are unreliable — document flow pattern directly and follow local protocol.
Formula — Pulsatility Index
PI = (Peak Systolic Velocity − End Diastolic Velocity) ÷ Time-Averaged Mean Velocity

INTERGROWTH-21st mean PI (Drukker 2020):
Mean PI = 1.02944 + 77.7456 × GA⁻² − 0.000004455 × GA³
(GA in completed weeks as decimal)
